Race Day Strategy

Start the first 3km at 5:50/km — slightly slower than goal pace. The urge to go faster will be strong because of the crowd and adrenaline. Resist it.

Km 4–15: settle into 5:38–5:42/km. Check your watch at every km but don't panic about individual splits.

Km 16–21.1: if you have anything left, this is when to use it. Each km faster than 5:41 banks ~5–6 seconds on your goal.

Fuel: take water at every station. If the race has gels, take one around km 9–10 even if you don't feel like you need it.

Non-Run Days

You have 5 non-run days per week. You don't need to fill them with cardio — your two quality runs are doing the work. If you want to add something:

Best add-ons: 20–30 min walks (great for recovery), yoga or mobility (your hips and calves will thank you), or strength work (single-leg squats, glute bridges, calf raises — prevents injury).

Avoid: hard cycling or swimming sessions on the day before your long run.

When Things Go Wrong

Missed a run: Skip it, don't double up. One missed run is nothing over 18 weeks.

Feeling off on a long run: Slow down by 30 sec/km. A slow long run beats a skipped one.

Niggle or pain: Take 3 easy days. If it persists, see someone. Don't run through pain in training — the race isn't worth an injury.

Taper madness (weeks 16–18): Your legs will feel heavy and weird. You might feel slow. This is normal. The fatigue is clearing. Trust the plan.
